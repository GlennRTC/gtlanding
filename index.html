The Artist

A Life Lived in Music

Born in Caracas, Venezuela, Glenn Humberto Tomassi began his musical formation at the Conservatorio José Ángel Lamas in 1972. His exceptional talent earned him a New York State half-scholarship to the LaGuardia High School of Music & Art and Performing Arts — the city's most prestigious public arts institution — where he studied under the legendary Joseph Allard.

A scholarship then took him to Berklee College of Music in Boston, where he completed his Bachelor of Music (1984) under Joseph Viola, followed by a Master of Music in Jazz Studies at the Manhattan School of Music (1986), returning to Joseph Allard's tutelage at the graduate level.

Over four decades, Tomassi built a career that defies simple categories: a studio musician who appeared on 30+ commercial recordings; a live performer who shared the stage with Ella Fitzgerald, Dizzy Gillespie, and Barbra Streisand; and an educator whose students now populate professional orchestras and international conservatories. A Yamaha artist endorsee in Venezuela, he served as Dean of Instrumental Music at the Colegio Internacional de Caracas for two decades, shaping a generation of Latin American woodwind artists.

"I work with each artist in an easy, relaxed manner — developing their full potential, taking into account their musical interests and tailoring the curriculum to their needs."
— Glenn Tomassi, Teaching Philosophy
